Air Matters

Adaptive Indoor Planting System For Enhancement of IAQ (Indoor Air Quality)

Spatial service design

Bachelor thesis & Individual work
2017.02-2017.06
Collaboration: GIGA Environmental Consultancy
Introduction

Background
The serious problem of IAQ (Indoor Air Quality) is often ignored by us. However, We spend about nearly 80% a day on indoor activity, and indoor air quality can be 3-5 times worse than outdoor air. Improving indoor air quality is far more urgent to some extent.

Design challenge
How to improve indoor air quality with an environment-friendly intervention?

My solution
Create an adaptive indoor planting system to address the indoor air problems. Also, I set up a web platform for guiding people to DIY build up that system with low cost and easy methods, and share their DIY experience.

General Strategy

I generally draft the ecosystem of my solution. First, I came up with a Modularized Planting Box for indoor space, it should be environment-sustainable, Open Source for DIY, and Adaptive to different interior spaces. Then, I envisioned an Open Information Platform, for people to get guidance (PGC) of installation building guidance and IAQ advice, as well as for people to share their personal DIY experience.

Experiment process
Implement my solution

Then, it is time to validate whether the planting box solution can exert a positive effect on air quality. So, I decided to implement my solution in a real space. I selected a lab in my college (BiDL Lab) and did a detailed spatial analysis. This is a super tiny space with a serious problem of western sunlight exposure and latent air pollution. I also conducted two experiments to figure out the temperature difference problem caused by the strong western sunlight, and figure out how much plant is needed in this room.

Solution part 2: a Web Platform

Then, the next question is, how to engage more people building up and applying this solution at their own home or office? I propose an UGC & PGC online community in which people can get professional IAQ knowledge and exchange their DIY solutions and help each other. I propose an Offline to Online development process which is divided into 3 phases. The development initiated from an offline campaign and this design project, and then, gradually, an online ecosystem came into being. I design the branding of the online community and name it: airmatters.






This web platform combining PGC and UGC which can help people (1) get information from the web about IAQ and plating knowledge. (2) do initial DIY build-up in aid of the online tool. (3)Share their online experience and mutually help other online community members.
