Ultimaker & CuraFlow

Vision for future mass production with 3d printers


Group Work (Floris Jansen, Fabiana Tomasini, Ronja Strikkers, Jaeho Sung, Mei Yanni)    
Tutor: Gert Pasman, Sjoerd van Dommelen     Tool: Adobe XD     Skill: Interaction Design

Introduction

Ultimaker 3D printers are mostly used for prototyping models on the carpet floor like fab labs or design offices. Now, Ultimaker is expanding its business area to the concrete floor like production lines in factories. In this project, we are redesigning the 3D printers managing system for factory manufacture.

Vision & Design

Vision Senario & Interaction



Context

The context we focused on is Concrete Floor, consisting of manufacturer factories with larger amounts of printers, which are used for production, manufacturing purposes. In this context, 10 to 40 printers should be managed by several operators.


Target Group

The target group in this project is 3D printing operators. Their task is making a printer fleet run smoothly and efficiently. Usually they get 3d model files from designers and engineers, and assign printing jobs to printers. Their job includes doing basic physical maintanence, switching printing material filaments, etc.


Besides 3d printers, they are also in charge of other production machines such as CNC millers. Thus, they need to move around the whole factory floor, between 3d printers, different machines and desktop.


In conclusion, Flexibility and Mobility essential priority for them.

Initial research

Based on the interviews and user inspections of the analysis phase of this project, we gathered a list of interactions and experience problems that we summarized in 3 main painpoints:

[1] The overlaying product ecosystem structure

Ultimake Product Ecosystem

Cura, Cura Connect, Ultimaker printers and Ultimaker app are all relevant products relevant to printing process, developed by Ultimaker. They share similar names, different 'locations' (on the user's computer, the Ultimakers themselves and the user's phone, respectively) and overlapping functionalities. This easily cause confusion for user to choose the right tool for a specific task. Ideally, this distinction does not matter to the user, to make sure the whole process of printing job is a fluent process.

[2] Insufficient depth of feedback

Ultimaker has its own desktop printing management software: Cura Connect. Also, they develop Ultimaker app for same function. However, this products over-simplify the printing process and give too few details and notifications when problems occur. Ideally, the products provide enough information for people to solve problems and take over control without having to consult the manual or online resources.

[3] The large quantities of printers

The current print job management interface is currently designed for only a small amount of printers. With more than about ten printers, it becomes harder to find and manage the right printer and print jobs. Ideally, the user can find the printer immediately.

Design exploration - Concept divergence



Initial Concept

We decided to design two concepts: one focused on a desktop interface and the other focused on a mobile phone interface. They mostly differ in ways of giving the operators a feeling of control. On the desktop, we assumed, they have more control, because they have more overview on a bigger screen. On the phone, we assumed, they have control because they will be able to change settings and abort a print whenever and wherever they are.

In order to efficiently develop the concept and gain more insight, we choose one essential part of printing process and develop prototype. This part is "seleting and grouping available 3D printers". This agile development enable us to quickly do concept test and iterate.

Concept#1 - Desktop

Interface

Scenario

"Group Overview" & "Printer Overview", these two main tabs are set for users fluently switch between production line and individual group. Additionally, an extra filter tab enable users to filter the list of printers based on the configurations they are looking for. The filter function can also be used when grouping the printers. The grouping is done by clicking "make new group"selecting the right printers and then clicking "group".

Concept#2 - Mobile

Interface

Scenario

The app is designed in such a way that you could use it to scan printers to group them, instead of having to select them on-screen. This way, users can physically see the printers they are grouping and the printers can give immediate feedback. Furthermore, the app could be used to get notifications of maintenance and/or faulty prints, so the operator can abort the print anywhere if necessary.

Comparative Concept Test

To compare two concepts and look for the gap between our understanding in context and our ideas, we conducted a comparative concept test. Via this test, we find out pros and cons of both concepts and explore essential design elements which are relevant to our proposed user experience.

Research Question

  • What are the pros and cons of both concepts?
  • How are they relavent to design goal: Efficiency and Feel of Control?

Test Method

We use paper prototype of interface and Ultimaker printers for this test. During test, we let participants to experience the context by explaining the storyboard and interact with the mock-up. Then, they were asked to perform a specific task: Create a printer group. In this process, we made video and audio recording for qualitative data, also, we user word clouds and System Usability scale for quantitative data.

Conclusion

In conclusion, participants prefer desktop model for its efficiency and clear overview. On the other hand, mobile interaction is preffered because participants can control printers anywhere and anytime, also they like seeing direct feedback of user impact from physicial printers.



In this phase, we explore the design ideas by generating two design concepts, and performed a comparative study. The aim of this exploration is to concluding the requirements and insights for the final design decisions.

Essential Design Decision

From concept comparative test and more research, most important decisions are made, which are highly relevant to our final design.

Tablet Application

Based on conclusion of comparative test, mobility and clear overview are two significant requirement in this context. Thus, a mobile application more fit this scenario. In that case, we need to move part of desktop functionalities (e.g. the print schedule) to the mobile application. This meant that a bigger screen is required. To keep an overview of the amount of necessary information, while keeping the functionality of a mobile device, we decided to design the app for a tablet. According to Ultimaker, a significant amount of companies already use company tablets in the operation of factory machines. Therefore, it is a reasonable medium to base our design on.

Thus, a new product is lauched: "Cura flow". Also, this decision lead to a new simplified ecosystem overview.

Schedule-based Interface + Color-coded Task List

Keeping track of what the printers are doing, is one of the main tasks of the future operator. Therefore, we made the schedule the main screen of the application. To make sure the users recognize it as a schedule, similar use cues as existing calendar and scheduling applications were used. Furthermore, we only showed information needed to be able to identify a job.

Main Schedule Interface




Additionally, keeping track of everything need to be done is an important requirement of operators. Hence, a clear tasklist with color coding is added.

Usability Test

In usability test, To test the usability and qualities of our concept, we went to a few companies to test our prototype with operators in real context of factory workshop. In this text, we raised following research questions:

  • Which interaction element impede people from finishing task? How serious is it?
  • Which interaction element should be priorited for better experience?
  • Do the people feel like the product matches the context they work in?

Method

This time, digital prototype and carboard 3D printer are utilized in this test. Participanted were show 3d printing video to get more enriched experience, and then were asked to perform three tasks: (1) create a new production line (2) take out finished model (3) fix error of printer (clogged nozzle).

Test in factory workshop

Analysis (Qualitative)

For qualitative data, we collected relevant quotes from the audio recordings. When we were not sure what the participant was talking about or whether they were completely open and honest, we checked the notes on body language and actions, and the video recordings. We cluster them based on its relevance between each other and figure out more deep and abstract meaning behind the quotes.

Analysing relevant quotes

Then the quoted were divided into 3 categories. What we more focused on is the 10 usability problems.

Qualitative results

Analysis (Quantitative)

For quantified analysis, we focus on error rate data. We checked the prototype screen recordings for usage patterns and collected all the error interactions in every step of each task.

1. Data collection about
usage pattern while testing

2. Collect errors on interface

3. Categorize the errors and
count times of try for the task completion



These errors were categorized into four levels: 'first try', 'second try', 'third try or more', and 'guidance needed to finish task'. We made an overview of the error occurrence of every single step of the three tasks. Since participants of the four tests differentiated a lot in knowledge about 3D printers and digital interface, we found that the test results were non-consistent. But we still managed to conclude useful insights.

Qualitative Analysis Result

Result

Finally, we prioritized the 10 usability problems based on the severity and frequency and put them in a graph. With severity, we mean the level of the negative impact the problem has on being able to successfully finish a task. With frequency, we mean the number of times the user comes across the task in their daily work. Based on the frequency-severity graph, we made an ordered list of the usability problems that need solving.
