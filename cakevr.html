Cake_VR

A socialVR tool for the remote co-design of customized cakes


Master thesis & Individual work, 2020.02-2020.08
Tutor: Huib de Ridder, Pablo Cesar, Jie Li     Tool: Blender, Unity, Photon     Skill: Qualitative research, VR development

My task: In this personal project, I conducted literature review, user research, ideation, VR development, user validation and analysis by myself.

Introduction

Cake_VR is a socialVR tool, allowing cake makers and clients to collaboratively design unique cakes remotely in a shared virtual space. It is the Master thesis of Design for Interaction, collaborating with CWI (Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica), national research institute for mathematics and computer science in the Netherlands.

Why socialVR, co-design and cake?



In this project, I want explore how to integrate co-design methodology to customization services to increase customer participation, and hence, user satisfaction. However, in the current situation, clients communicate about cake design with pastry chefs in face-to-face meeting, followed up by online chat. The current way is insufficient for the users to convey and understand abstract design ideas. Therefore, I think SocialVR is a promising medium to support remote co-design activities, for its interactive 3d visualization and free operation.


SocialVR is defined as a VR-based social interaction technology, which allows multiple users who are physically separated to interaction with each other in a shared virtual space.


Co-design involves customers into the design process and decision-making of the product development, together with professional designers.

Research Procedure



This project followed a coherence research-through-design process. Literature review and user research were conducted to inform the design concept and requirements. After that, The design concept was implemented, into a form of medium-fidelity VR prototype. The prototype was validated by the user tests, to investigate the impacts of socialVR tool on co-design activities, as well as evaluate to what the text the design concept was realized and implemented.

User research



In this research, I wanted to understand the current interaction of the customized cake services . Here, I want to explore two questions. In order to answer these questions, I interview 5 cake makers and 4 clients who have personalized cakes before.

  • How do clients and cake makers generate ideas and make the design decisions?
  • What's their concerns and latent needs?

I transcripted the interview audio recordings and collected the relevant quatations. Then, I conducted a thematic analysis by clustering these quatations. After that, I conducted two main categories: (1) Personal experienece of bakers and clients (2) Communication workflow. This information led to two research results.

[Result 1] workflow of baker-client communication

The workflow is generally divided into three stages: Giving client input (Custmer convey their intial thoughts to inform the cake design); Ideation & Negotiation (Bakers and customers colloratively develop the cake ideas by negotiation); Agreement (Bakers document the final decisions for reaching an agreement)

First, the idea is originated from client inputs. In this phase, client express their ideas to cake maker, based on the emotion they would convey via this cake, or the context in which the cake would be consumed, or, the desired physical features of the cake. When describing their ideas, clients usually utilize a collection reference images to illustrate the abstract thoughts.

Second, the cake idea is developed based on the collaborative ideation and negotiation. Cake makers and clients adapt some reference design templates, combine existing design elements, or create something from scratch. They exchange ideas and professional advices, in aid of diverse media, such as dummy, photos and sketch or even real cakes. These media were efficient in presenting tacit information, such as size, 3d shape, aesthetic, which are difficult to express in words.

Third, the client and cake maker reach an agreement on the final decision by making a conclusion. They usually transform all the design details into texts and images, and then documents them into a digital or physical contract, as an evidence of their consensus.





[Result 2] Concern/Latent needs to Design requirements

  • There is a lack of integration of multiple media in the design process.
  • Participants lack immediate and direct perception of the effects of their design intervention.
  • A time-efficient method is needed for cake makers to embody the finalized cake ideas.
  • There is a need for presenting the finalized ideas including both vivid details and an overall image.
  • Cake makers need a more efficient way to manage the decision information so as to conclude design decisions.

Based on these results as well as the current state of art in literature, I concluded 10 design requirements of the CakeVR.

Design



Based on the design requirements, I drafted the general system of CakeVR. It is a virtual sapce where two users can meet each other represented as cartoonish avatars by wearing HMDs. They can switch the virtual environment between a virtual bakery and an event context where the cake would be consumed. Opertation interfaces were offered for them to do 3d works, and the 3d models will be visualized real-time.

Also, all the ten design requirement are included in the proposed interaction flow of CakeVR. Here is the video demo in which all of them will be elaborated.

VR development



The development process was generally divided into three phases: [1] 3d modelling in Blender; [2] VR interaction development in aid of C# for Unity; [3] Multi-user connection and data sync by PhotonPun.



1. 3d modelling and shading and UV shading in Blender, to create the 3d shapes and visual texture of the mostly user cake component models.

2. 3d manipulatin, UI interaction and the upper body motion of avatar is developed with C# in Unity, together with Oculus integration.

3. PhotonPUN is used to connect the multiple users via the server, and syncronize the 3d data, motion and function calls between the users.

User Evaluation



Then, this prototype was evaluated for exploring two research questions.

  • How does this socialVR tool affect users’ collaborative and social behavior in co-designing customized cakes, compared with face-to-face communication?
  • Do the design requirements fix users’ latent needs in the cake co-design activities?

Test Setup

A dual-user test was performed including one participant (play the role of client) and one researcher (play the role of cake maker). In CakeVR, they need to collaboratively design a cake for a specific context (i.e., event, time, location) which are generated by the participants themselves.




The procedure of the test is divided into 3 phases. [1] A skype interview is performed one day before the VR test, for inspiring the participants to generate a context for which the cake would be made. The outcome of the interview is a collection of reference images and key words which would present the cake idea. [2] These reference images will be loaded into the VR application for the formal VR co-design sessions.[3] A post-test interview will be performed, and the participants were asked to fill in the System Usability Scale questionnaires (SUS).




The VR tests were performed in the researcher’s studio. In order to simulate remote communication, a physical separation was set between the two users. They communicate via skype wearing a noise-cancelling earphone.

Data Analysis

Obersevation

I did an observation study by watching the screen-captured video of the participant’s view in VR, in order to investigate their behavior pattern.

Thematic analysis

Additionlly, I did thematic analysis by clustering the significant quatations in interviews, to investigate their experience with regard to social interactin and 3d work in co-design.

Result 1 - Added value of socialVR in co-design

1. EFFICIENCY OF COMMUNICATION

The efficiency of communication about cake ideas was highly improve due to the real-time 3d manipulation and embodiment. Collaborators can quickly edit the cake to show and evaluate their design ideas, and quicky reach consensus.

2. SHARED UNDERSTANDING

All the participants claimed that CakeVR helped to build up better shared understandings of the design process and fianl decisions between collborators, due to the abundant visual evidence (i.e., images, 3d models), interative 3d visualization as well as intuitive dectic guestures with hand or lase pointers.

3. EASY SYSTEM

All the participants confirmed that the system, especially the gesture-based 3d manipulation was quick to learn and easy to use. This conclusion is also supported by the results of System Usability Scale.

Result 2 - Limitation of socialVR

1. Limited creative resources

All the participants thought that the preset model in the toolbar was insufficient for the free design work. One participant claimed that he want to get access to the Internet search engine for more inspiring resources.

2. Insufficient fidelity of design outcome

Although some participants thought the the fildelity of the final design outcome was not sufficient enough for them to percieve the cake ideas in a detailed way, some other participants thought that such fidelity was sufficient for them to get a general concept of the aesthetic and configuration of the cake.

3. Affective interaction and co-presence

The lack of facial expression and emotional perception in virtual avatar leads to different user experience on participants who have different needs for emotional connection in collaboration,. For people who have higher requirements, they were negatively influenced, and hence, less engaged. For people who have lower needs, there are little impact, or positive user experience.

Result 2 - validate design requirements

We validate each requirements by asking two questions, with regard to the user experience and utility of the design requirements. The result shows that 80% of the design requirement are helpful to the co-design work, and four of them are realized in a good quality.







I also investigated the system usability. Participants evaluated the usability of this tool by filling in the SUS form, they were encouraged to think aloud while filling in the questionnaires.

Recommendation for study methods

1. More Samples. The test sample should be larger, recruiting participants with diverse backgrounds and personal features. In addition, we hope to invite participants who truly need to personalize cakes in the real world. From user tests, we discovered that it was difficult to fully contextualize participants because they didn’t really need to order a customized cake.

2. Involve professional cake makers. In this VR co-design test, the role of cake maker was played by the researcher who had little experience in cake making and business negotiation. There was a gap between real bakers and researchers with regards to professional knowledge and negotiation skills. In addition, since the opinions of professional cake makers on the socialVR tool were lost, we failed to investigate how this tool affects design experts’ behavior in the co-design activities and whether this tool meets their needs.

3. Different assignments. In general, all the contexts generated by participants are emotional and personal (i.e., birthday party), and this might have an impact on their experience and behavior in the design process. We assume that such a design process might be different from designing a cake for a result-oriented context (i.e., customize a cake for an anniversary of X company with a budget of 100 euro). Therefore, it is advisable to perform a comparative test and situate the participants in a result-oriented context.




Recommendation for prototype

1. Design for dialogue. Face-to-face communication.

2. Design for realism. Higher rendering quality to improve environment realism.

3. Design for inspiring environment. A more creative environemnt encouraging people to play with 3d models.
